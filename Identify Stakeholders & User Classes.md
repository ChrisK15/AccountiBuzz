# Stakeholder Analysis & User Class Identification

## Primary User Personas

### Sarah: The Competitive Achiever

**Demographics & Characteristics:**
- 28 years old, urban professional in marketing
- Fitness enthusiast who participates in CrossFit and running events
- High achiever with multiple concurrent goals
- Tech-savvy, early adopter of productivity tools
- Income: $85,000+
- Highly active on social media, particularly Instagram

**Primary Needs:**
- Competition-driven accountability with visible metrics
- Rigorous verification methods to ensure authenticity
- Multiple accountability groups organized by specific goals
- Recognition for achievements and progress
- Data visualization to track performance over time

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Training for a half marathon while maintaining strength training
- Seeking external validation and recognition
- Motivated by comparing performance to others
- Desires objective proof of accomplishment
- Wants to be part of a high-performing community

**Pain Points:**
- Becomes demotivated when unable to see others' progress
- Frustrated by accountability partners who aren't serious
- Dislikes apps that don't have robust verification
- Loses interest if there's no competitive element

**Technology Usage:**
- iPhone power user with multiple fitness apps
- Wears Apple Watch or Fitbit continuously
- Comfortable with video recording and sharing
- High daily screen time (4+ hours)

### Marcus: The Consistency Seeker

**Demographics & Characteristics:**
- 34 years old, remote software developer
- Lives in suburban area with home office setup
- Values work-life balance but struggles with boundaries
- Methodical, process-oriented personality
- Income: $110,000
- Limited social media presence, prefers direct communication

**Primary Needs:**
- Streak maintenance and visual progress tracking
- Supportive community that celebrates consistency
- Screen recording or simple verification methods
- Clear boundaries between work and personal goals
- Collective progress indicators over individual competition

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Maintaining consistent work hours and preventing burnout
- Building lasting habits around deep work and focused time
- Motivated by unbroken streaks and progressive improvement
- Desires supportive accountability without judgment
- Wants to be part of a community with shared values

**Pain Points:**
- Extremely sensitive to breaking streaks
- Becomes discouraged by overly competitive environments
- Struggles with work-from-home distractions
- Dislikes complex or time-consuming verification processes

**Technology Usage:**
- Dual-monitor setup with Android smartphone
- Uses productivity tools like Notion, Todoist
- Comfortable with screen recording and screenshots
- Moderate daily screen time (primarily work-related)

### Jessica: The Flexibility-First Parent

**Demographics & Characteristics:**
- 39 years old, part-time consultant with two children (ages 5 and 8)
- Lives in suburban area with unpredictable schedule
- Values work-life integration and family priorities
- Pragmatic, adaptable personality
- Income: $65,000 (part-time)
- Moderate social media usage focused on family and close friends

**Primary Needs:**
- Grace periods for life's unpredictability
- Simple verification methods that don't add stress
- Supportive, judgment-free accountability
- Privacy controls and emotional safety in groups
- Flexible goal tracking that accommodates interruptions

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Creating consistent self-care routines amid family responsibilities
- Building sustainable habits that fit around parenting
- Motivated by supportive community and personal progress
- Desires accountability that acknowledges real-life constraints
- Wants to be part of a community that understands parenting challenges

**Pain Points:**
- Frustrated by rigid systems that don't accommodate interruptions
- Anxious about judgment when life circumstances prevent goal completion
- Overwhelmed by complex verification requirements
- Dislikes intrusive notifications or pressure tactics

**Technology Usage:**
- iPhone user with limited time for apps
- Often uses devices in short bursts between responsibilities
- Prefers simple interfaces with minimal learning curve
- Low-to-moderate daily screen time (2-3 hours)

## User Class Definitions

### Primary User Classes

#### Power Users (15% of user base)
- **Description**: Highly engaged users who create and manage multiple accountability groups
- **Characteristics**: Daily active usage, high verification compliance, multiple goal tracking
- **Representative Persona**: Sarah (Competitive Achiever)
- **Key Requirements**: Advanced customization, detailed analytics, leadership tools

#### Core Users (60% of user base)
- **Description**: Regular users who actively participate in accountability groups
- **Characteristics**: 3-5 days weekly usage, consistent verification, 1-3 active goals
- **Representative Persona**: Marcus (Consistency Seeker)
- **Key Requirements**: Reliable streak tracking, supportive interactions, moderate customization

#### Casual Users (25% of user base)
- **Description**: Intermittent users who participate selectively based on availability
- **Characteristics**: 1-3 days weekly usage, variable verification compliance, 1-2 active goals
- **Representative Persona**: Jessica (Flexibility-First Parent)
- **Key Requirements**: Flexible verification, grace periods, supportive environment

### Secondary User Classes

#### Group Administrators
- **Description**: Users who create and manage accountability groups
- **Characteristics**: Cross-cut primary classes, higher engagement with management features
- **Key Requirements**: Group customization tools, member management, activity insights

#### Verification Validators
- **Description**: Users who verify others' goal completion (in certain group types)
- **Characteristics**: Trusted members, higher engagement with verification features
- **Key Requirements**: Streamlined review process, clear verification guidelines

#### New Users
- **Description**: Recently joined users in first 30 days
- **Characteristics**: Learning platform, establishing habits, exploring features
- **Key Requirements**: Intuitive onboarding, early wins, guided feature discovery

## Conflict Analysis Between User Needs

### Primary Tensions

#### 1. Verification Rigor vs. Accessibility
- **Conflict**: Sarah requires rigorous verification while Jessica needs simple, low-friction options
- **Resolution Strategy**: Implement tiered verification options that groups can select based on their focus, with default settings aligned to group type
- **Technical Consideration**: Verification system must support multiple media types and validation workflows

#### 2. Competition vs. Support
- **Conflict**: Sarah thrives on competition while Marcus and Jessica prefer supportive environments
- **Resolution Strategy**: Create toggle controls for competitive features at the group level, with clear indicators of group type during discovery
- **Technical Consideration**: Leaderboard and ranking systems must be modular and configurable per group

#### 3. Streak Rigidity vs. Flexibility
- **Conflict**: Marcus values unbroken streaks while Jessica needs accommodation for life interruptions
- **Resolution Strategy**: Implement grace period system with customizable allowances per group and visible "perfect" vs. "maintained" streak designations
- **Technical Consideration**: Streak calculation logic must support multiple interpretations of continuation

#### 4. Privacy vs. Visibility
- **Conflict**: Sarah wants public recognition while Jessica requires privacy controls
- **Resolution Strategy**: Granular privacy settings at both user and content levels, with clear consent flows for shared achievements
- **Technical Consideration**: Content sharing architecture must respect complex permission models

#### 5. Notification Frequency vs. Boundaries
- **Conflict**: Sarah welcomes frequent engagement while Jessica needs minimal interruptions
- **Resolution Strategy**: User-defined notification preferences with schedule-aware delivery and group-specific settings
- **Technical Consideration**: Notification system must support complex scheduling and batching rules

### Secondary Tensions

#### 1. Onboarding Depth vs. Simplicity
- **Conflict**: Power users want comprehensive setup while casual users need quick start
- **Resolution Strategy**: Progressive onboarding with essential first steps and optional advanced configuration
- **Technical Consideration**: User settings must have sensible defaults with clear paths to customization

#### 2. Group Discovery vs. Privacy
- **Conflict**: Some users want to find new groups while others prefer private participation
- **Resolution Strategy**: Opt-in discovery with clear privacy indicators and controlled invitation mechanisms
- **Technical Consideration**: Group visibility settings must be enforced across all discovery touchpoints

#### 3. Data Richness vs. Overwhelm
- **Conflict**: Data-driven users want detailed insights while others find them overwhelming
- **Resolution Strategy**: Layered data presentation with progressive disclosure of detailed metrics
- **Technical Consideration**: Analytics dashboard must support multiple views with appropriate defaults per user type

## User Needs Mapping to System Requirements

### Verification System
- **Must support**: Video uploads, screen recordings, photos, text check-ins
- **Must enable**: Group-specific verification requirements
- **Must provide**: Streamlined upload process with appropriate compression
- **Should include**: Verification templates based on goal type

### Group Management
- **Must support**: Customizable competitive features (on/off)
- **Must enable**: Privacy controls at group and user levels
- **Must provide**: Clear group type indicators during discovery
- **Should include**: Group-specific verification and streak rules

### Streak and Progress Tracking
- **Must support**: Traditional streak counting with visual indicators
- **Must enable**: Configurable grace periods (2-3 days/month)
- **Must provide**: Celebration of milestones regardless of perfection
- **Should include**: Distinction between perfect and maintained streaks

### Notification System
- **Must support**: Schedule-aware reminders with quiet hours
- **Must enable**: Custom notification preferences by group and type
- **Must provide**: Accountability partner communication with boundaries
- **Should include**: Intelligent reminder timing based on past completion patterns

### User Experience Customization
- **Must support**: Interface simplification options
- **Must enable**: Feature visibility toggles for different user preferences
- **Must provide**: Consistent navigation regardless of customization
- **Should include**: Persona-based default settings with easy modification

This stakeholder analysis and user class identification will guide feature prioritization, interface design, and technical implementation throughout the development process. By addressing the specific needs of our primary personas while resolving key tensions between their preferences, DailyPact will deliver a highly personalized accountability experience that adapts to diverse user needs.
